Introduction: Acute compartment syndrome (ACS) is a limb-threatening complication of extremity trauma that requires timely diagnosis to prevent irreversible muscle and nerve injury. Current diagnostic methods are invasive, intermittent, and operator-dependent. We evaluated the feasibility of a novel, Bluetooth-enabled electrical impedance myography (EIM) device (mAlert, Myolex, Inc., Brookline, MA, USA) for continuous, noninvasive detection of ACS-related tissue changes.
