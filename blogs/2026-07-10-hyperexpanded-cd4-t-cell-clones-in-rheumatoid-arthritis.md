Objective: Clonal hyperexpansion of CD4 T cells is a characteristic feature of rheumatoid arthritis (RA). Equally large T cell clones also arise in physiological ageing or latent viral infection and adopt a replicative senescence programme - a tolerance mechanism that limits immune activation by innate-like reprogramming and proliferative arrest. We aimed to characterise the senescence programme of hyperexpanded CD4 T cell clones in RA and to define their clinical associations.
